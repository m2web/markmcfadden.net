The City of Man, on the other hand, represents the earthly, temporal realm characterized by human institutions, including governments and politics. Augustine argues that while Christians live in and interact with the City of Man, their ultimate loyalty is to the City of God, and they must prioritize their spiritual obligations over earthly ones.
